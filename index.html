What does “r” mean?
“r” measures how closely two things move together across your logged days. It runs from −1 to +1.
+r
Move together. When one goes up, the other tends to go up too, e.g. more sleep, better mood.
−r
Move opposite. When one goes up, the other tends to go down, e.g. more stress, worse sleep.
≈ 0
No clear link. The two don’t follow each other in your data.
−1 strong opposite0 none+1 strong together
Rule of thumb: below 0.2 is little to none, 0.2–0.4 weak, 0.4–0.6 moderate, above 0.6 strong. And remember: correlation isn’t causation.
